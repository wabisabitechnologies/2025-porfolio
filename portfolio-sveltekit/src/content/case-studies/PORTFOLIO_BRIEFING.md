## The Three Flagship Stories

### **Story 1: Roles & Permissions - Governance as System**

**Problem:** Who can do what? (Manual matrix that everyone ignores)

**SFL's Approach:** Role-based access control with permission templates tied to company structure

**Why It Matters for You:**
- Shows you understand authorization systems
- Demonstrates how constraints (roles, permissions) enable rather than restrict
- Proves you can design scalable access patterns
- Connects to practitioner scalability (one role template = works for 100 people)

**Recruitment Value:** "This person understands distributed authorization at enterprise scale"

---

### **Story 2: Minute Book & Registers - Compliance as Byproduct**

**Problem:** Scattered statutory records = compliance nightmare

**SFL's Approach:** Business actions automatically create register entries

**Why It Matters for You:**
- Shows you understand regulatory requirements deeply
- Demonstrates how to automate compliance without manual checkboxes
- Proves you can design consistency constraints (all registers stay in sync)
- Connects to risk reduction (immutable audit trails)

**Recruitment Value:** "This person understands regulatory systems and can automate them"

---

### **Story 3: Application Review & Approval - Workflow Orchestration**

**Problem:** Multi-party approvals are serial bottlenecks

**SFL's Approach:** Parallel approvals with smart routing

**Why It Matters for You:**
- Shows you understand workflow orchestration
- Demonstrates how to handle complexity without exposing it to users
- Proves you can optimize business processes
- Connects to scalability (faster decisions = more transactions)

**Recruitment Value:** "This person can design elegant systems for complex workflows"

- Demonstrates: Deep market knowledge, regulatory empathy, local context understanding

### **2. Practitioner-Centric Design**
- Not built for C-suite executives
- Built for: Company secretaries, compliance officers, legal practitioners
- Problem solved: How do practitioners scale from managing 5 to 50 companies?
- Demonstrates: Empathy for end users, scalability thinking, professional services understanding

### **3. Systematic vs. Automated**
- Not just "automate the busywork"
- Actually: "Turn compliance into business logic so it runs automatically"
- Key insight: Compliance that's built-in > compliance that's enforced
- Demonstrates: Architectural thinking, regulatory understanding, systems design

### **4. Data Consistency as First-Class Feature**
- Not just storing data
- Actually: Enforcing consistency across registers (share transfer updates both ledger AND register of members)
- Key innovation: Atomic transactions for business logic
- Demonstrates: Database design, transaction integrity, attention to detail

### **5. Audit Trail as Always-On**
- Not "audit trail as an add-on"
- Actually: Every action timestamped, attributed, immutable by default
- Key insight: Compliance proof is automatic if you design for it
- Demonstrates: Regulatory thinking, security mindset, quality culture
